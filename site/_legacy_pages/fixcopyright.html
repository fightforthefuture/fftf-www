On Nov 16, the Republican Party published a memo
calling for strong copyright reform:

" Current copyright law does not merely distort some markets… …rather it destroys entire markets. "

" Copyright violates nearly every tenet of … capitalism "

" …corporate welfare that hurts innovation and … consumers "

" It is a government-subsidized monopoly "

" Too much copyright protection … will greatly stifle innovation "

Within 24 hours, they pulled it.
Now, they've fired Derek Khanna, the guy who wrote it.

Hey Congress, don't let MPAA and big content scare you off -- our copyright system is getting in the way of free speech, art, and innovation. It’s time to fix it.

The Republican copyright memo said things about copyright that most people hold to be true. Copyright is supposed to be a means of promoting art and innovation, but now it's strangling both. Videos get censored over tiny bits of music, accidental infringers get sued for more than the value of their house, and liability threatens the innovation behind our high-tech economy.
